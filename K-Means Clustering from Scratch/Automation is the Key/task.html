Description

Remember we told about the silhouette score in the previous step? It is now time to use it, too, to double-check the k you determined in the previous step.

We need you to use the sklearn.metrics.silhouette_score to calculate the score for different k. Since the higher the silhouette score is, the better the clustering is (for our case at least), we need to determine the maxima.

Do the same range for k you did in the previous step. In case the k you get is the same as at the previous step, you should feel safe about your choice.

Objectives
  1. Use the custom K-Means class with k ranging from $2$ to $10$ (both included) and calculate the silhouette score for each k.
  2. Print the results for the silhouette score for each of the k values.
  3. To find the desired k, plot the dependence of the silhouette score from k and determine the maxima. Check if it is the same k as you had in the previous step.
